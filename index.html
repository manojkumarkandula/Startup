Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about patent protection and our services.

How long does the patent process typically take?

The patent process timeline varies by country and technology field. In India, it typically takes 2-3 years from filing to grant. Our accelerated processes can sometimes reduce this timeframe. International patents may take 3-5 years. During this time, your application receives "patent pending" status, which provides some protection and market advantage.

What makes an idea patentable?

For an idea to be patentable, it must meet three primary criteria: novelty (being new and not previously disclosed), non-obviousness (not an obvious extension of existing technology to someone skilled in that field), and utility (having a practical application). Additionally, the innovation must fall within patentable subject matter as defined by the specific country's patent laws.

How much does patent protection cost?

Patent costs vary based on complexity, technology field, and geographic coverage. In India, a basic patent application typically costs ₹50,000-₹2,00,000 including government fees and attorney services. International protection costs more, with each country adding ₹3,00,000-₹5,00,000 on average. We offer flexible payment plans for startups and academic institutions to make protection more accessible.

Should I keep my invention secret before filing?

Yes, maintaining confidentiality before filing is crucial. Public disclosure of your invention before filing can jeopardize your patent rights in most countries. While India and the US offer a grace period (12 months in India, 12 months in the US), many other countries require absolute novelty. We recommend using NDAs for any necessary disclosures and consulting with us before sharing your innovation publicly.

What's the difference between a patent, trademark, and copyright?

These three protect different aspects of intellectual property: Patents protect inventions, functional designs, and processes, typically lasting 20 years. Trademarks protect brand identifiers like logos and names, and can be renewed indefinitely with proper use. Copyrights protect original creative works like books, music, and software code, lasting for the author's lifetime plus 60 years in India. Many innovations benefit from a combination of these protections.
