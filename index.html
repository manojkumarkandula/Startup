Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our patent filing process.

How long does the patent process take?

The patent process timeline varies based on the type of protection you choose. A provisional patent can be filed within 2-3 days of submission. For a full utility patent, the drafting and filing process typically takes 4-5 days. After filing, USPTO examination can take 1-3 years, though you have patent pending status during this time.

What's the difference between provisional and full patents?

A provisional patent application establishes an early filing date and gives you "patent pending" status for 12 months while you develop your invention. It's less formal and less expensive. A full (non-provisional) utility patent provides complete protection for 20 years and includes formal claims, detailed descriptions, and professional illustrations. Many inventors start with a provisional and then file a full patent before the 12-month period expires.

How do you protect my idea during submission?

All submissions are automatically protected under our legally binding NDA. Our secure platform uses bank-level encryption, and access to your information is strictly limited to the patent attorneys assigned to your case. We never share your ideas with third parties without your explicit permission.

What if my idea isn't patentable?

If our assessment determines your idea may not be patentable in its current form, we'll provide detailed feedback on why and suggest potential modifications that could improve patentability. We may also recommend alternative forms of intellectual property protection, such as trademarks, copyrights, or trade secrets. If we determine your idea is not patentable at all, we offer a partial refund of our service fee.

Do your fees include government filing fees?

Our service fees include our legal work, patent drafting, and filing services. USPTO filing fees are additional and vary based on your entity size (small entity, micro-entity, or large entity). We'll provide a clear breakdown of all applicable government fees before filing, and you can pay these directly through our platform. Most individual inventors and startups qualify for reduced fees as small or micro entities.
